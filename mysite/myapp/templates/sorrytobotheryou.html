The movie starts off inoccuously enough with the main protagonist being a regular guy trying to find a job to help
provide for his family. After this the movie slowly transitions to the primary moral dilemma of the film which is
the protagonist having to choose between joining his friends and sticking to his morals or getting a promotion at
work (for a company that sells weapons and promotes "slavery"). The protagonist initially chooses his job over
friends, girlfriend, and ideals. While the movie tries to make it look like the protagonist is in the wrong and give
his friends, protesters the moral high ground it instead comes off as making them look immature, and jealous of the
protagonist's newfound success. During this time the movie attempts to inject humor by adding some random, forced
scenes such as a tv show where the contestant getting beatup is the primary selling point. As the protagonist proceeds
working at his now high paying job he very gradually and predictably predictably questions his choice. The protagonist
however doesnt "learn" his lesson until his job forces him into ridiculous extremes including discovering enslaved horse
people in the basement of his boss's home. Overall Sorry to Bother you is a movie that tries to satirize black identity
and corporate ethics, but comes off flat due to a slow and predictable plot as well as boring characters. It is a movie
that does a lot of things including comedy, drama, sci-fi, and politics but does a mediocre job at each. And while there
are a few chuckle worthy moments in the film I wouldn't recommend it for most people.

Rating: 6/10
